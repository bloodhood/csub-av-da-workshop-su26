# Preliminary Findings Report

## Key Findings

### Finding 1 — Age Is the Strongest Predictor
Stroke patients are on average **26 years older** than non-stroke patients (67.7 vs 41.9 years). The relationship is highly statistically significant (p < 0.0001). Senior patients (>60) have a stroke rate of 13.57% compared to 0.13% in patients under 30.

### Finding 2 — Hypertension Triples Stroke Risk
Patients with hypertension have a stroke rate of **13.25%** vs 3.97% without. This represents a 3.3x increase in risk. The association is highly significant (Chi-Square p < 0.0001).

### Finding 3 — Glucose Level Is a Strong Risk Factor
Stroke patients have significantly higher average glucose (132.5 vs 104.8 mg/dL). Patients in the highest glucose quartile (>114 mg/dL) have a stroke rate of **10.9%** vs 1.9% in the lowest quartile.

### Finding 4 — Work Type Differences Are Significant
Self-employed patients have the highest stroke rate (7.94%), nearly twice the rate of Private employees (5.09%). Children and Never_worked have near-zero rates, largely explained by age.

### Finding 5 — High-Risk Patients Are Identifiable
A composite risk score (0–5) successfully identifies high-risk patients. Those with a score of 3+ have an **18.24% stroke rate** — nearly 4x the population average.

## Significant Relationships Found
- Age vs Stroke — SIGNIFICANT (p < 0.0001)
- Hypertension vs Stroke — SIGNIFICANT (p < 0.0001)
- Heart Disease vs Stroke — SIGNIFICANT (p < 0.0001)
- Glucose Level vs Stroke — SIGNIFICANT (p < 0.0001)
- Work Type vs Stroke — SIGNIFICANT (p < 0.001)
- BMI vs Stroke — SIGNIFICANT (p = 0.0014)

## Unexpected Observations
- BMI alone is a weaker predictor than expected — stroke risk peaks at Q3 (moderate obesity), not Q4 (extreme obesity)
- Smoking status showed no statistically significant association (p = 0.079), possibly due to 30% "Unknown" values masking the true relationship
- Gender showed no significant difference in stroke rates despite general clinical assumptions

## Business / Clinical Implications
1. Age-based screening programs would capture the majority of high-risk patients
2. Hypertension management is the single most actionable clinical intervention
3. Glucose monitoring programs should target patients with avg_glucose > 114
4. A simple 5-factor risk score could be implemented in any clinic's EHR system
