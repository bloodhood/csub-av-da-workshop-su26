# Research Narrative

## What Story Does the Data Tell?

The healthcare stroke dataset tells a clear and actionable story: **stroke is not random — it is predictable**.

The data shows that a small set of clinical and demographic factors — age, hypertension, heart disease, and glucose level — account for the overwhelming majority of stroke risk. A 70-year-old patient with hypertension and high blood glucose is dramatically more likely to experience a stroke than a 35-year-old with none of these factors.

Specifically:
- Patients over 60 account for **71% of all strokes** in this dataset, despite representing only 25% of patients
- Just three factors (age > 60, hypertension, glucose > 140) together identify a subgroup with stroke rates 4–5x higher than the overall population
- The remaining 95% of the population has relatively low and manageable risk

The story arc: most patients are low risk, but a clearly identifiable high-risk group is not being adequately screened or treated.

---

## What Should Stakeholders Learn?

**Healthcare providers** should learn that a simple 5-factor risk score can be implemented at the point of care to flag high-risk patients automatically. No expensive testing required — age, blood pressure status, heart history, glucose, and smoking history are already collected at routine visits.

**Public health departments** should learn that stroke prevention resources are most efficiently deployed to the 60+ demographic, particularly those already managed for hypertension or diabetes.

**Patients** should learn that their most modifiable risk factor is glucose control. While age cannot be changed, blood pressure and glucose levels can be managed through medication and lifestyle changes.

---

## What Actions Should Stakeholders Take?

1. **Implement a stroke risk scoring tool** in electronic health records — automatically flag patients with score >= 3 for stroke risk counselling
2. **Launch a 60+ annual screening program** — prioritize patients with any comorbidity (hypertension, heart disease, or high glucose)
3. **Establish glucose thresholds** — patients with avg_glucose > 140 should receive targeted dietary and pharmacological interventions
4. **Improve smoking status data collection** — 30% "Unknown" status reduces the power of smoking as a predictor; better data would improve the risk model
5. **Partner with self-employed and private sector employers** — these groups show the highest stroke rates among working-age adults
