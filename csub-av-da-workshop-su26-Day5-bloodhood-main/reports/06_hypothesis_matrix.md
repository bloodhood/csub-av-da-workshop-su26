# Hypothesis Matrix

## Hypotheses for All Research Questions

| RQ | Null Hypothesis (H0) | Alternative Hypothesis (H1) | Test | Result |
|----|---------------------|----------------------------|------|--------|
| RQ1 | No difference in age between stroke and non-stroke patients | Stroke patients are significantly older | T-Test | REJECT H0 (p < 0.001) |
| RQ2 | Hypertension has no relationship with stroke | Hypertension significantly increases stroke risk | Chi-Square | REJECT H0 (p < 0.001) |
| RQ3 | Heart disease has no relationship with stroke | Heart disease significantly increases stroke risk | Chi-Square | REJECT H0 (p < 0.001) |
| RQ4 | Smoking/work type has no effect on stroke rates | Lifestyle factors significantly affect stroke rates | ANOVA + Chi-Square | REJECT H0 (p < 0.05) |
| RQ5 | No difference in glucose levels between groups | Higher glucose significantly increases stroke risk | T-Test | REJECT H0 (p < 0.001) |
| RQ6 | No difference in BMI between groups | BMI significantly differs between stroke groups | T-Test | FAIL TO REJECT H0 (p > 0.05) |
| RQ7 | Residence type has no effect on stroke rates | Urban/Rural residence affects stroke risk | Chi-Square | FAIL TO REJECT H0 (p > 0.05) |
| RQ8 | Marital status has no relationship with stroke | Marital status is related to stroke risk | Chi-Square | REJECT H0 (p < 0.05) |
| RQ9 | No gender difference in stroke rates | Gender significantly affects stroke rates | Chi-Square | FAIL TO REJECT H0 (p > 0.05) |
| RQ10 | No patient segment has elevated combined risk | Specific patient segments have significantly higher stroke risk | Segmentation | Confirmed — Senior + hypertensive group |

## Significance Level
**Alpha (a) = 0.05** across all tests (95% confidence level)

## Key: 
- **REJECT H0** = Statistically significant relationship found
- **FAIL TO REJECT H0** = No statistically significant relationship found
