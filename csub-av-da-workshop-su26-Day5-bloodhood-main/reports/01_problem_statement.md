## Real-World Problem
Stroke is the second leading cause of death globally and a major cause of long-term disability. In the United States, someone experiences a stroke every 40 seconds, and someone dies from a stroke every 4 minutes. Early identification of high-risk individuals can significantly reduce stroke incidence through timely medical intervention.

## Why This Problem Is Important
- Approximately 795,000 people experience a stroke each year in the US
- Stroke costs approximately $34 billion annually in healthcare and lost productivity
- Up to 80% of strokes are preventable with proper risk factor management
- Identifying at-risk patients enables targeted prevention programs that save lives

## Who Are the Stakeholders?
| Stakeholder | Interest |

| Public Health Departments | Design population-level prevention programs |

| Hospital Administrators | Allocate resources for stroke care and prevention |
